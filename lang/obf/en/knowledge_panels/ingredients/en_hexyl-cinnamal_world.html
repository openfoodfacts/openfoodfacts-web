• What it is: A synthetic fragrance ingredient with a floral, chamomile-like scent. Also known as hexyl cinnamic aldehyde.
  • Why it's used: Used as a fragrance component in perfumes, lotions, and other scented cosmetic products.
  • Concerns:
  • One of the 26 allergens that must be listed separately in EU cosmetics if present above certain thresholds.
  • Can cause allergic contact dermatitis and skin sensitization.
  • Classified as a skin sensitizer by the Scientific Committee on Consumer Safety (SCCS).
  • Sources: European Commission, SCCS
