• What it is: An anionic surfactant derived from ethoxylated lauryl alcohol. A milder alternative to sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS).
  • Why it's used: Primary cleansing agent and foaming agent in shampoos, body washes, and facial cleansers.
  • Concerns:
  • Generally considered safe for rinse-off products.
  • Can be drying or irritating to skin and eyes, especially at high concentrations.
  • Potential for contamination with 1,4-dioxane during manufacturing; reputable manufacturers minimize this.
  • Less irritating than SLS but still can strip natural oils from skin and hair.
  • Sources: Cosmetic Ingredient Review, FDA
