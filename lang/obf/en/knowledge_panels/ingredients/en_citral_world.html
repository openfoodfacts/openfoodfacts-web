• What it is: A naturally occurring aldehyde found in lemongrass, lemon myrtle, and citrus oils. Has a strong lemon scent.
  • Why it's used: Used as a fragrance ingredient to provide fresh, citrus notes in products.
  • Concerns:
  • One of the 26 allergens that must be listed separately in EU cosmetics if present above certain thresholds.
  • Can cause allergic contact dermatitis and skin sensitization.
  • May cause photosensitivity in some individuals.
  • Oxidizes readily, becoming more allergenic over time.
  • Sources: European Commission, Contact Dermatitis Journal
