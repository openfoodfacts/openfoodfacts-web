• What it is: Mineral oil, a clear, colorless liquid petroleum byproduct. Highly refined and purified for cosmetic use.
  • Why it's used: Acts as an emollient and occlusive agent, creating a moisture barrier on skin. Prevents trans-epidermal water loss.
  • Concerns:
  • Cosmetic-grade mineral oil is safe when highly purified.
  • Non-comedogenic in refined forms despite myths to the contrary.
  • Petroleum-derived, raising sustainability concerns for some consumers.
  • Very stable and doesn't oxidize or become rancid.
  • Avoid industrial-grade; only cosmetic-grade is safe for skin.
  • Sources: Cosmetic Ingredient Review, FDA
