• What it is: A synthetic surfactant derived from coconut oil and dimethylaminopropylamine. Amphoteric surfactant that is gentle on skin.
  • Why it's used: Acts as a foam booster, viscosity builder, and mild cleansing agent. Often used in shampoos and body washes to create rich lather.
  • Concerns:
  • Generally well-tolerated and considered gentle.
  • Can cause allergic contact dermatitis in some individuals.
  • Contamination concerns with dimethylaminopropylamine (DMAPA) in some products.
  • Rated as a moderate hazard ingredient by EWG due to potential contamination.
  • Sources: Cosmetic Ingredient Review, Contact Dermatitis Journal
