• What it is: Processed foods, such as bottled vegetables, canned fish, fruits in syrup, cheeses and freshly made breads, are made essentially by adding salt, oil, sugar or other substances from Group 2 to Group 1 foods.
  • Processing methods: Processes include various preservation or cooking methods, and, in the case of breads and cheese, non-alcoholic fermentation. Most processed foods have two or three ingredients, and are recognizable as modified versions of Group 1 foods.
  • Purpose: The purpose of processing here is to increase the durability of Group 1 foods, or to modify or enhance their sensory qualities.
  • Usage: They are edible by themselves or, more usually, in combination with other foods.
