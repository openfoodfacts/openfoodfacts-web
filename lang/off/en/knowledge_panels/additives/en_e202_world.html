Potassium sorbate (E202) is a synthetic food preservative commonly used to extend the shelf life of various food products.

It works by inhibiting the growth of molds, yeast, and some bacteria, preventing spoilage. When added to foods, it helps maintain their freshness and quality.

Some studies have shown that when combined with nitrites, potassium sorbate have genotoxic activity in vitro.

Two recent large-scale epidemiological studies (NutriNet-Sant√© cohort, 2025) have suggested links between its consumption and increased risks of chronic diseases: * A 14% increase in overall cancer incidence and 26% increase in breast cancer was observed in highest consumers compared to non-consumers. * A 115% increase in type 2 diabetes risk was observed in highest consumers compared to lowest consumers.

These results require confirmation by other studies, but are consistent with experimental work on potential mechanisms (microbiota disruption, oxidative stress).
