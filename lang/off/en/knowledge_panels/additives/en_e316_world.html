Sodium erythorbate (E316) is a synthetic antioxidant frequently used in the food industry.

It is commonly added to processed meats (which contributed to 42.3% of intake in the NutriNet-Santé cohort) to prevent oxidation, maintain color, and extend shelf life.

Two recent large-scale epidemiological studies (NutriNet-Santé cohort, 2025) have suggested links between its consumption and increased risks of chronic diseases: * A 12% increase in overall cancer incidence and 21% increase in breast cancer was observed in highest consumers. * A 43% increase in type 2 diabetes risk was observed in highest consumers compared to lowest consumers.

These results require confirmation by other studies, but are consistent with experimental work suggesting mechanisms of oxidation, oxidative stress, and inflammation.
