Key Research Areas

Our data supports diverse research domains

Nutrition & Public Health

Researchers use our database to study nutritional quality, food composition, and public health interventions. Open Food Facts data has been instrumental in Nutri-Score research and evaluation.

Environmental Impact

Studies on carbon footprint, Eco-Score, and environmental sustainability of food products benefit from our comprehensive database of product information.

Food Processing

Research on ultra-processed foods using the NOVA classification relies on our detailed ingredient lists and processing information.

Computer Science & AI

Machine learning, image recognition, natural language processing, and data science projects leverage our open datasets and images.

Consumer Behavior

Studies examining food choices, labeling effects, and transparency in the food industry use Open Food Facts data.

Food Policy

Policy makers and researchers analyze food regulations, labeling requirements, and industry practices using our transparent database.

Resources for Researchers

How to use and cite Open Food Facts

Accessing the Data

Open Food Facts provides multiple ways to access our data:

  • Database exports: Download complete database dumps in various formats. See our Data page for details.
  • API: Query our database programmatically via our comprehensive API. API Documentation
  • Search interface: Explore products directly on our website with advanced search and filtering capabilities.

Citing Open Food Facts

If you use Open Food Facts in your research, please cite it appropriately. Here's a suggested citation format:

Website citation:

Open Food Facts. (Year). Open Food Facts - World. Retrieved from https://world.openfoodfacts.org


Dataset citation:

Open Food Facts. (Year). Open Food Facts Database [Data set]. Retrieved from https://world.openfoodfacts.org/data

Licensing

Our data is open and freely reusable:

  • The database is available under the Open Database License (ODbL)
  • Database contents are under the Database Contents License (DbCL)
  • Product images are available under Creative Commons Attribution ShareAlike (CC BY-SA 3.0)

See our Terms and conditions of use and re-use for complete details.

Data Quality

As a crowdsourced database, data quality can vary. When using Open Food Facts for research:

  • Consider filtering by completeness scores or data quality indicators
  • Validate critical data points if precision is essential
  • Be aware of geographical and temporal biases in our data coverage
  • Contact us if you need help with data quality assessment for your research

Collaborate with Us

We welcome collaboration with research teams:

  • Data needs: Let us know if you need specific data or improvements to our database structure
  • Scan parties: Organize targeted data collection campaigns for your research region or product category
  • Results sharing: Share your findings with our community and help advance knowledge
  • Tool development: Collaborate on tools that benefit both researchers and consumers

Contact us at contact@openfoodfacts.org to discuss collaboration opportunities.
